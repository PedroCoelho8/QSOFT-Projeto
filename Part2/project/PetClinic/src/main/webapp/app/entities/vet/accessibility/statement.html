Accessibility Statement for Pet Clinic

This is an accessibility statement from ISEP.

Measures to support accessibility

ISEP takes the following measures to ensure accessibility of Pet Clinic:

  • Include accessibility as part of our mission statement.
  • Assign clear accessibility goals and responsibilities.

Conformance status

The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) defines requirements for designers and developers to improve accessibility for people with disabilities. It defines three levels of conformance: Level A, Level AA, and Level AAA. Pet Clinic is partially conformant with WCAG 2.1 level AA. Partially conformant means that some parts of the content do not fully conform to the accessibility standard.

Technical specifications

Accessibility of Pet Clinic relies on the following technologies to work with the particular combination of web browser and any assistive technologies or plugins installed on your computer:

  • HTML
  • WAI-ARIA
  • CSS

These technologies are relied upon for conformance with the accessibility standards used.

Limitations and alternatives

Despite our best efforts to ensure accessibility of Pet Clinic , there may be some limitations. Below is a description of known limitations, and potential solutions. Please contact us if you observe an issue not listed below.

Known limitations for Pet Clinic:

  1. Buttons do not have an accessible name: Buttons may not have text alternatives because Buttons don't have the aria labels inside when they were created. We will start adding ARIA Labels into the buttons. Contact us if you encounter an issue.
  2. Colours do not have a sufficient contrast ratio: The background and foreground colors in certain areas of the application do not meet the required contrast ratio, making the content less legible for users, especially those with visual impairments. because Its because of poor color selection. We will start adjusting the colours for a better compatibility between them. Contact us if you encounter an issue.
  3. Links do not have discernible names: Certain links do not have discernible names, reducing their semantic clarity and usability for users relying on assistive technology.because Lack of links with non-descriptive text. We will start use ARIA Labels for non-text links. Contact us if you encounter an issue.
